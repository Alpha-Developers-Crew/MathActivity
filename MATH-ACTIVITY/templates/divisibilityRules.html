Divisibility Rules

A divisibility rule is a simple method or shortcut used in mathematics to check whether one number is divisible by another number without actually doing the division.

Divisibility Rules

2 :- If the last digit of the number is even then the number is divisible by 2

3 :- If the sum of the digits of the number is divisible by 3 then the number is divisible by 3

4 :- If the last 2 digits of the number is divisible by 4 then the number is divisible by 4

5 :- If the last digit of the number is 0 or 5 then the number is divisible by 5

6 :- If the number is even and divisible by 3 then the number is divisible by 6

7 :- The divisibility rule of 7 states that you should take the last digit of the number, double it, and then subtract that result from the rest of the number. If the final result is a multiple of 7 or 0, the original number is divisible by 7.

8 :- If the last 3 digits of the number is divisible by 8 then the number is divisible by 8

9 :- If the sum of the digits of the number is divisible by 9 then the number is divisible by 9

10 :- If the number has 0 at its units place then the number is divisible by 10
